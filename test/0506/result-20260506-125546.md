## App-owned browser launch test

Script:

```powershell
.\test\0506\run-device-runner-sbrowser-once.ps1 -Serial 172.30.1.78:35013 -DeviceName z1
```

Outputs:

- `device-runner-sbrowser-once-20260506-125303.*`
- `device-runner-sbrowser-once-20260506-125418.*`

Result:

- App-owned launch worked.
- Activity stack showed:

```text
com.navertraffic.samsung/.ui.MainActivity
-> com.navertraffic.samsung/.strategy.SamsungBrowserLaunchActivity
-> com.sec.android.app.sbrowser/.SBrowserMainActivity
```

- The app logged 1st and 2nd mobile Naver search URL opens.
- MID tap failed in app-owned mode.

Reason:

- `BrowserAccessibilityService` is enabled and bound.
- Samsung Internet web content is not exposed in the accessibility node tree enough for the app to verify and click the matching MID/title.
- This means app-owned launch can match the "external app opens browser" shape, but cannot currently prove "MID-matched product tap" without a DOM/DevTools inspection path.

This proves:

- 1st search opened.
- 2nd search opened.
- Matching MID anchor was found as `tracked-search-gate`.
- Physical tap was issued.

- Detail swipe ran.

Gap:

- The likely reason is that `/json` page polling sees page-level URLs and can miss very fast intermediate redirects.
